
“There’s too many foreigners,” said Lesley Gardner, 74, a retired lorry driver. “I used to start at 5am and finish at 11am for £100. The foreigners will do the same for £40. I believe leaving will increase the wages again.”

“I am not concerned about them coming and working, but I am concerned about the social effects,” said Peter Massam, 48, a petrol tanker driver. “They are not interested in integration, it’s harder to get GP appointments, and I had a problem getting my child into primary school.”
